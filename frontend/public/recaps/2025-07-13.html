🥇 Performance of the Week

It pains me to even type this, but Abel Tavera decided to show up and torch the field with a ridiculous +7 and rack up 30 points. No one wants to see this man on fire, mostly because he's so damn loud about it. He also scooped over $200 and then vanished faster than a fart in the wind so he didn't have to buy a single beer. Not that it matters—he probably won't even read this recap anyway. Good thing he's too scared to play Jason anymore, saved me some cash.

🫠 Worst Performance of the Week

Jeff Morris came crashing down to earth after a record low of 74 last week. Word from his group was that he took 'chubby' to an entirely new tier after that mid-round encounter with the pink spandex badonkadonk. Apparently when all the blood rushes south, your swing goes straight to hell. A –11 meltdown after that little rendezvous. Some guys can't handle their booze, others can't handle an ass you could park a Buick on.

📈 Other Notables

  • Jamey's Birthday Edition – Bryan Guy's was the day before, but this round was all about Jamey trying to outdrink common sense.
    • He knocked back 7-8 shots, a few beers, and a couple of double transfusions like it was a corner store special and he had coupons. He said he was heading to see Superman, but last I heard he was still in row G, half-passed out and mumbling about shanks, Henny, and pink cakes to a confused usher.
    • Standing over a 20-foot birdie putt on 18, Jamey slurred, "I can't even see the ball." We told him to hit the one in the middle, and damned if he didn't drain it. Maybe he should play blind more often.
    • Rumor has it that he came stumbling out from behind a tree on the wrong fairway, hat cockeyed, casually mumbled "hey guys," and zig-zagged back to his group like it was the most normal thing ever.
    • Most of the round, he was hypnotized by the big booty in pink spray-on Lululemons. She was chipping his balls in more ways than one. I left for the green after hearing her say "I need a hotdog," and you can imagine how that conversation spiraled.
  • Bryan Guy is hoarding CTPs again, snagging another just as Q thought he was catching up. Keep grindin', Q. I'm just grateful I was there to see the legends in action.
  • Jeff Shorts managed to lip out 8 putts all by less than an inch, and somehow played a round with zero pars but a birdie. On 18, he missed an eagle by two inches (or an Adam Shorts as we call it). Some would call that bad luck, I call it Sunday.
  • Welcome (or welcome back?) to David Harris of PGC fame, who wasted no time in introducing himself by taking our money with a big shit-eating grin. Welcome to the party!
  • Jason Newberg debuted his new $1,000 putter he wouldn't shut up about. Unsurprisingly, his 3-putt average only climbed higher. Turns out it's not the wand—it's the wizard. Who knew?
  • Abel vaulted up 5 spots and cracked the top 10 for the first time all season. See, all it takes is one good week to make the rest of us question our life choices.

🌴 Course Conditions

Bardmoor sucks. Water seems to lurk perfectly wherever your ball wants to go, and the greens were like a Tinder date—looked smooth from a distance but up close, it was all bumps and disappointment. Aside from a few anomalies, the place chewed up everyone's scorecard.

GCHL Rule 3e: If you are not able to finish a round due to injury or weather (and everyone must quit), your score doesn't count and you don't have to pay for the money games. Don't worry Dovid, I've got your $24 entry ready for next time. Get well soon.

USGA Rule 5.5b: The player may practise putting or chipping on or near:

  • The teeing area of the next hole.

But such practice strokes must not be made from a bunker and must not unreasonably delay play (see Rule 5.6a).
